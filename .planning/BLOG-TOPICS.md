# Seaduced Journal — 10 Launch Topics

## Research Method

Web search synthesis across three audience arcs: (1) self-care-routine seekers, (2) perimenopausal / menopausal women navigating hormonal change, (3) LGBTQ+ intimate-wellness readers. Topics were scored against five criteria plus Julian's added **Buying Emotion** criterion (what state the reader is left in that makes them WANT a sea-moss-infused personal lubricant) — see scoring notes below each row.

Ranking is **combined** share-worthiness, save-worthiness, on-brand fit, emotional driver strength, and (heavily weighted) the specific path from article's closing emotion → purchase intent.

Never medical advice. Educational angle only.

## Notes on Ranking Logic

- **Topics 1–3** are ranked highest because each combines strong validation/permission with a direct emotional path to the bottle — the reader closes the article and the *feeling* of wanting the product is already in their body, not just in their head.
- **Topic 4 (ingredient literacy)** is ranked ahead of the hero-ingredient piece (5) because it activates buyer's-remorse energy toward competitors, which is a very short bridge to a Seaduced purchase.
- **Topics 7 & 8** ranked below 1–6 because their emotional payoff is slightly slower to convert — they deepen brand affinity rather than triggering an immediate ritual-craving, but they are foundational for retention and sharing.
- **Topic 10** is highly save-worthy (reference-like, long half-life) but ranks last on buying emotion because it leaves the reader in analytical mode, not desire mode. Still a Journal staple.

## Content Principles (apply to every post)

1. **No medical claims.** Educational and experiential only. Footnote research where relevant.
2. **Inclusive voice.** Never assume body, partner, age, or orientation. When specific, specify — don't default.
3. **Body-positive.** Desire and change are framed as expansion, never as problems to fix.
4. **Clean, editorial voice.** Mid-length sentences, no hype adjectives, no emoji, no exclamation points except in the tagline zone.
5. **Tailored CTA.** Every article's `ArticleCTA` matches the emotion just created (e.g. post-ritual article → "Start your ritual" not "Shop Now").
